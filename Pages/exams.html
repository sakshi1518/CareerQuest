Exams at a Glance

  • Andhra Pradesh Engineering, Agriculture, and Medical Common Entrance Test

    Eligibility: 10+2 | Notification Month: March

    APEAMCET is conducted for admission to various undergraduate courses such as B.E./B.Tech, B.Pharmacy, B.Sc (Agriculture), B.Sc (Horticulture), B.V.Sc & A.H, B.F.Sc, and B.Tech (Bio-Technology) in the participating institutions.After or appearing 10+2 students or its equivalent with specific subjects as per the course requirements are eligible to apply for APEAMCET.The exam is conducted in an online mode (Computer Based Test). It typically consists of multiple-choice questions (MCQs) with a total of 160 questions (for Engineering) and 160 questions (for Agriculture and Medical). The duration of the exam is 3 hours. Each correct answer is awarded one mark, and there is no negative marking for incorrect answers.Based on the ranks obtained in APEAMCET, candidates are called for counseling and seat allotment process. Seats are allotted to candidates in various colleges and universities based on their preferences and availability of seats.

  • Joint Entrance Examination Main (JEE Main)

    Eligibility Criteria: 10+2 with PCM | Notification Month: September-November,February

    Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), this is the primary entrance exam for admission to prestigious engineering institutes like the India Institutes of Technology (IITs) and the National Institutes of Technology (NITs) GFTIs (Government Funded Technical Institutes), and various other engineering colleges across the country. JEE Main is typically conducted twice a year, usually in January and April. Students can appear for both attempts, and the best score among the two is considered for admission. The subjects covered in JEE Main include Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.Each correct answer is awarded four marks for MCQs and numerical value-based questions, while there is a penalty of one mark for each incorrect answer in MCQs. No negative marking is applicable for numerical value-based questions.

  • JEE Advanced:

    Eligibility Criteria: 10+2 with PCM and Qualify JEE Main | Notification Month: April

    JEE Advanced is another national-level entrance exam conducted in India. After qualifying the JEE Main, candidates can appear for the JEE Advanced to seek admission to the IITs. JEE Advanced is usually held once a year.

  • Common Law Admission Test (CLAT)

    Eligibility Criteria: 10+2 | Notification Month: February-March

    Conducted for admissions to undergraduate and postgraduate law programs in various National Law Universities (NLUs) and other participating institutes in India. CLAT is one of the most popular law entrance exams and is conducted annually by the Consortium of National Law Universities. CLAT scores are accepted by 22 National Law Universities across India and several other private and government law colleges. CLAT is a computer-based test (CBT) consisting of multiple-choice questions. The undergraduate CLAT exam includes questions from subjects like English, General Knowledge, Mathematics, Legal Aptitude, and Logical Reasoning. The postgraduate CLAT exam includes questions on law-related topics.The CLAT scores are valid for admission to the participating institutes for the current academic year only.

  • Kerala Engineering Architecture Medical (KEAM)

    Eligibility Criteria: 10+2 With PCB | Notification Month: February-March

    It is a state-level entrance examination conducted by the Commissioner of Entrance Examinations (CEE), Kerala, India. The exam is primarily for admission to various undergraduate professional courses, including Engineering, Architecture, Medical, Agriculture, Forestry, and Veterinary courses offered by government and private colleges in the state of Kerala. KEAM is a two-part examination. Part I includes Physics and Chemistry, while Part II includes Mathematics (for Engineering and Architecture courses) or Biology (for Medical, Agriculture, and related courses). The exam is conducted in offline (pen and paper) mode.Based on the KEAM scores and rank, candidates are invited for the counseling process. During counseling, candidates can exercise their preferences for college and course, and seat allotment is done based on the merit and availability of seats.

  • National Defence Academy (NDA)

    Eligibility Criteria: 10+2 | Notification Month: February

    It is a prestigious institution in India that trains young individuals to become officers in the Indian Armed Forces. It is a joint services academy where candidates are trained for the Army, Navy, and Air Force. NDA is located in Khadakwasla, Pune, and is one of the most sought-after career options for those aspiring to join the armed forces. Both male and unmarried female candidates can apply for NDA, but only male candidates are eligible for permanent commission in the Indian Armed Forces.The age limit for NDA varies depending on the specific course and entry. Generally, candidates must be between 16.5 to 19.5 years of age.Admission to NDA is through a two-stage selection process. The first stage is a written examination conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC). The exam consists of two papers: Mathematics and General Ability Test (GAT). Candidates who clear the written exam are called for the second stage, which includes a series of interviews and physical fitness tests conducted by the Services Selection Board (SSB).Selected candidates undergo three years of training at NDA, where they receive academic and military training. The training is comprehensive and includes subjects like Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, History, Geography, and other general topics. Cadets also receive rigorous physical training, leadership development, and exposure to various outdoor activities.

  • National Entrance Screening Test (NEST)

    Eligibility Criteria: 10+2 With PCM/PCB | Notification Month: March

    It is conducted for admission to the National Institute of Science Education and Research (NISER), Bhubaneswar, and the University of Mumbai - Department of Atomic Energy Centre for Excellence in Basic Sciences (UM-DAE CEBS), Mumbai. NEST is an important examination for students who wish to pursue integrated M.Sc. programs in the fields of Biology, Chemistry, Mathematics, and Physics at these prestigious institutions.NEST is jointly conducted by NISER and UM-DAE CEBS, two premier research institutes in India dedicated to science education and research.The NEST examination is designed to assess the candidate's aptitude and understanding of science subjects at the 10+2 level. The syllabus includes topics from Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, and Biology.NEST is a computer-based test (CBT) consisting of five sections - General, Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, and Biology. The questions are of multiple-choice type, and the exam is conducted in English.

  • National Aptitude Test in Architecture (NATA)

    Eligibility Criteria: 10+2 With PCM or 3yr Diploma | Notification Month: February

    It is conducted by the Council of Architecture (COA), which is the statutory body responsible for regulating architectural education in the country.NATA is accepted by various colleges and universities offering undergraduate programs in Architecture across India. It is the mandatory aptitude test for admission to the B.Arch. (Bachelor of Architecture) course. NATA is conducted in two parts - Part A and Part B. Part A is a computer-based test (CBT) that includes multiple-choice questions (MCQs) related to mathematics, general aptitude, and architectural awareness. Part B is a drawing test where candidates have to create freehand sketches, draw objects and scenes, etc.The NATA score is valid for two years from the year in which the candidate appeared for the examination.
