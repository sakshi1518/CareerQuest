GOVERNMENT JOBS AFTER 10TH

  • Staff Selection Commission Multi Tasking Staff (SSC MTS):

    It is a competitive exam conducted by the Staff Selection Commission (SSC) in India to recruit candidates for various non-technical Group 'C' positions in various government ministries, departments, and offices. The SSC MTS exam is held annually, and it offers job opportunities for candidates who have completed their matriculation (class 10) or equivalent from a recognized board. The recruitment is done for positions that involve routine clerical, administrative, and support tasks in government organizations.

  • State Postal Recruitment for Gramin Dak Sevaks (GDS):

    State Postal Recruitment for Gramin Dak Sevaks (GDS) is conducted by the postal departments of individual states in India. Gramin Dak Sevaks are also known as Village Post Office Branch Postmasters, Mail Deliverers, and Mail Carriers. They are responsible for various postal services in rural areas. The recruitment for Gramin Dak Sevaks is conducted by the Indian Postal Department, specifically the Department of Posts (DoP), which operates under the Ministry of Communications, Government of India.

  • Indian Navy MR:

    The full form of Indian Navy MR is Indian Navy Matric Recruit. The Indian Navy MR Recruitment is conducted to recruit eligible candidates for the position of Sailor MR. The selection process for the Indian Navy MR includes a written exam, physical fitness test, and medical examination.
    Currently the application process for the Indian Navy Agniveer MR Recruitment is going on. The MR recruitment is being conducted online on the official website of the Indian Navy. Indian Navy MR [Agniveer] Recruitment is conducted to recruit eligible unmarried male and female candidates for the position of Agniveer (MR) under the Agnipath scheme.

  • Indian Army Soldier:

    An Indian Army Soldier, also known as a Jawan, is a member of the ground forces of the Indian Army, one of the largest and most prestigious armies in the world. The Indian Army is responsible for defending the nation's borders, safeguarding its territorial integrity, and ensuring national security.Everyone who has finished class 10th is qualified to join the Indian Army, regardless of their field of study. Candidates can join Indian Army after 10th in various posts such as General Duty Soldier, and Tradesman. Candidates will have to undergo several stages like document verification, physical measurement, physical fitness test, medical test, written exam, merit list, etc.
