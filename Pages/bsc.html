  • B.Sc-PCZ

    Bachelor of Science (B.Sc. PCZ) is a three-year undergraduate program that focuses on Zoology, with Physics and Chemistry as minor subjects. The course covers Animal Biodiversity, Biochemistry, Physiology, Cell and Molecular Biology, Ecology, Biotechnology, and Environmental Management. Graduates can pursue diverse career paths like animal biodiversity prospecting, bioinformatics, Indian Forest Service (IFS), environmental management, and more, with attractive salary packages. The annual fees range from INR 3000 to approximately INR 7,000 in most institutions, and private universities may charge higher fees up to INR 1 lakh.

  • B.Sc-Anthropology

    The BSc Anthropology course is an undergraduate program with a duration of 3 years. It offers full-time and distance education options and requires a minimum of 50% marks in 10+2 (Science stream) for eligibility. Admission is based on merit or entrance exams like SET, CUCET, CUET, and NPAT. The course fees range from INR 50,000 to 2 Lakhs. Graduates can pursue careers as researchers, cultural consultants, forensic anthropologists, etc. Top recruiters include SEWA, TISS, ICSSR, Ministry of Culture, ASI, and ICMR. After BSc, students can opt for MSc or PhD in Anthropology for further studies. Average salary in India is around INR 5 - 7 LPA.
